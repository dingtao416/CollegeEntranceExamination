# Ten Essay Notes

Use this file to borrow strategic strengths from ten widely circulated high-scoring gaokao essays. Do not copy plots, titles, or diction mechanically.

## 1. 赤兔之死

- Winning move: turn an abstract value prompt into a vivid fable-like narrative.
- Learn: personify an object or historical figure only when the moral logic is airtight.
- Avoid: fake classical diction that collapses after two paragraphs.

## 2. 怀想天空

- Winning move: trust plain detail and real labor scenes.
- Learn: make feeling emerge from sequence, action, and family texture.
- Avoid: replacing lived life with lyrical fog.

## 3. 穿越百年的芬芳记忆

- Winning move: connect historical memory to sensory imagery.
- Learn: keep lyric prose tethered to a clear emotional route.
- Avoid: image piles with no argumentative or emotional spine.

## 4. 站在黄花岗陵园的门口

- Winning move: use place as a moral and historical hinge.
- Learn: let the scene carry public feeling.
- Avoid: choosing a grand form when you only have grand tone.

## 5. 绿色生活

- Winning move: concretize a broad concept.
- Learn: turn big words into visible habits, settings, and judgments.
- Avoid: writing policy slogans instead of literature.

## 6. 与你为邻

- Winning move: start from near relations and widen toward shared social fate.
- Learn: make `邻` mean coexistence, dependence, and recognition.
- Avoid: staying at the level of polite surface morality.

## 7. 拒绝平庸

- Winning move: build a strong stance and maintain tonal confidence.
- Learn: make refusal specific. Refuse drift, imitation, dead habit, or dead language.
- Avoid: shouting `不平庸` without showing what an un-pingyong life looks like.

## 8. 忧与爱

- Winning move: handle a relation prompt dialectically.
- Learn: prove that the two terms are not opposed but mutually illuminating.
- Avoid: discussing each keyword separately with no bridge.

## 9. 庄子的智慧

- Winning move: bring classical thought into present life.
- Learn: cite one thinker deeply enough to show relevance, not just memory.
- Avoid: turning the essay into notes for a culture lecture.

## 10. 老爹

- Winning move: shape a whole person through trade, speech, and repeated object detail.
- Learn: let character and value emerge from craft and everyday action.
- Avoid: overpraising a parent figure in abstract language.

## Cross-essay patterns

- High-scoring essays usually answer the prompt early, even when they seem leisurely.
- They choose a narrow entry point and then widen naturally.
- They remember that `文采` is controlled force, not verbal excess.
- They either think clearly, feel truthfully, or both.
- They rarely depend on volume. They depend on pressure.

## Fast selection guide

Use a `怀想天空` or `老爹` path when the prompt can be grounded in family, labor, school, or place.

Use a `忧与爱` or `拒绝平庸` path when the prompt demands judgment and value articulation.

Use a `庄子的智慧` or `穿越百年的芬芳记忆` path when the prompt rewards culture and reflection.

Use a `赤兔之死` or `站在黄花岗陵园的门口` path only when your formal control is clearly above average.
